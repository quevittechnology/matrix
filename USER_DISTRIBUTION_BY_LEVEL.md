## üéØ Key Insights

### **1. Level Distribution Patterns**

**Conservative (40% at Level 1):**
- Most users stay at entry level
- Limited upgrade activity
- Lower total volume
- Smaller royalty pools

**Aggressive (20% at Level 1):**
- Strong upgrade culture
- Higher engagement
- Massive total volume
- Large royalty pools

### **2. Royalty Tier Competition**

**Tier 1 (Level 10):**
- Most users (2,500-20,000)
- Lowest per-user share
- Easiest to reach

**Tier 4 (Level 13):**
- Fewest users (300-3,000)
- Highest per-user share
- Most exclusive

### **3. Income Opportunities**

**Early Levels (1-5):**
- Large user base
- Frequent upgrades
- Good for building team

**Mid Levels (6-9):**
- Moderate user base
- Significant income potential
- Balance of volume and value

**Top Levels (10-13):**
- Small user base
- Highest individual earnings
- Royalty income bonus

---

## üí° Strategic Implications

### **For Users:**

1. **Level 5:** Minimum to start earning well
2. **Level 10:** Sweet spot for royalty (Tier 1)
3. **Level 11:** Often best per-user royalty share
4. **Level 13:** Maximum status and earnings

### **For Network:**

1. **More Level 1 users** = More referral income
2. **More Level 10+ users** = Larger royalty pools
3. **Balanced distribution** = Sustainable growth
4. **Active upgrades** = Higher total volume
